Brief History of SGA

Students united to form the Bryn Mawr College Self-Government Association in 1892. The College therefore became the first institution of higher education in the United States to give students responsibility to not only enforce rules of behavior upon themselves, but also for decide what those rules should be. The two primary institutions of student self-government at Bryn Mawr are the Self-Government Association (SGA), to which every student belongs, and the Honor Code, a set of principles stressing personal integrity and mutual respect to which all students agree to adhere.

Annual Events

Sunday Meetings - Everyone is welcome to attend SGA Meetings every Sunday at 7pm in the Campus Center Main Lounge. We discuss campus wide issues, student events, current events, and global occurrences.

Plenary is a time to spread awareness and gather as a collective to discuss resolutions to better life at Bryn Mawr. During this event, we reaffirm our commitment to self-governance, the SGA Constitution, and the Honor Code as a  community. Plenary gives us privileges such as SGA, allocation of funding student clubs, traditions, and self-scheduled exams.

Big Cheese Forum is an annual event where students gather to ask administration and staff questions regarding their roles and how they can best support students' needs going forward.

Committee Yourself occurs every September, November, & April and is an opportunity for students to apply and interview for the various committees on campus. There are two weeks within each month the event is held where members of the Appointments Committee choose not just good applicants but the right applicants for their respective roles.

Bring Your ¢hange is an even held each semester where students organize around the importance of giving back to the college. The Fall Semester involves an educational event regarding financial literacy on campus, and the Spring Semester event is centered around fundraising. Leaders on this campus hope that ultimately these events lead to an increased rate of participation in giving which allows for Bryn Mawr to receive greater financial aid packages, grants, and donations.
